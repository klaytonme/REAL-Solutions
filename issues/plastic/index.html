Single Use Plastics

The Dreaded Compound

Plastic is a problem.

We produce 300 million tons of plastic each year, and 150 million tons of that is single use! Single use plastics are any plastic product that is intended to be used once and then thrown away or recycled. Some examples of single use plastics are: plastic forks and knives, Styrofoam and plastic take out containers, and, of course, plastic straws. According to the U.N. Environment the single use plastics most found in the environment are: cigarette butts, plastic drinking bottles, plastic bottle caps, food wrappers, plastic grocery bags, plastic lids, straws and stirrers, other types of plastic bags, foam take-away containers.

Because petroleum based plastics aren’t biodegradable, they’re deposited in landfills. Over time, that plastic is buried in the ground or carried out into the ocean. Once it’s there, it begins to release the toxic chemicals from the additives used to form and shape the plastic. These chemicals make their way into our food and are now being found in our bloodstream, where they could cause many severe illnesses.

Here are some ways to reduce plastic waste in your life

  • Carry reusable shopping bags
  • Use a reusable cup in your coffee
  • Don't use plastic you don't need like straws
  • Recycle gum (it's plastic!)
  • Use glass or steel instead of plastic tupperware
  • Recycle correctly
  • Use a reusable water bottle
  • Buy clothing in natural fabrics

Renewable Drinkware

Don't Drink and Drive (the Planet into the Ground)

A large contributor to each person's plastic footprint (and a simple one to combat) is single use drinkware. Whether it's plastic cups, paper cups lined with plastic, plastic drinking straws, or any combination of those, it can be replaced with a waterbottle, reusable cups, and alternative material straws. Renewable Shopping bags

Reuse at the Register

Shopping with reuseable bags is not just better for the planet, but more convenient for everyone! Get ahold of some cloth bags and bring them with you to the store. Even paper shopping bags (available at many grocery stores) can serve many terms and be safely recycled when their time comes.
