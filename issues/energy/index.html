Energy Conservation

Because Our Planet is Running Out

Energy is a problem.

In 2019, the average electricity consumption for a U.S. household was 10,649 kilowatt hours (kWh). That’s enough energy to run a 100 watt lightbulb for over 100,000 hours, or eleven and a half years. In a state like Washington, where the vast majority of our energy comes from renewable sources, this might not be much cause for concern. However, the majority of our country still runs on non-renewable sources of power. In fact, also in 2019, only 11% of the nation’s energy consumption was from renewable sources: the other 89% was from non-renewable sources, primarily petroleum and natural gas. Because of this fact, it’s very important for individuals to reduce their energy consumption as much as possible.

There are a few ways to reduce your consumption every day! There are a few day-to-day behaviors that you can change for free, and some could even save you money. You can make sure to turn off lights and appliances when you aren’t using them, wash dishes by hand when you have time, and use heating and air conditioning as little as possible. You can also buy more energy efficient devices, such as LED or CFL lightbulbs or smart power strips. While energy efficient products might cost more initially, they make up that added cost on your utility bill. In fact, many appliances are marked with both an initial purchase price and an annual operating cost, which represents the cost of its typical energy consumption. By buying appliances with lower annual operating costs, you can reduce the energy consumption of every product you use.
