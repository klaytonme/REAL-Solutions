Fast Fasion

Clothing that Wears the Planet Back (Like Wears Down... I Know)

Fashion is a problem.

The cycle of fast fashion, buying new clothes cheaply, abundantly, and frequently are taking its toll on our planet. Microplastics from synthetic clothing fibers get stuck in the ocean, unable to break down. The fashion industry accounts for 10% of annual global carbon emissions. Making one pair of jeans produces the same amount of greenhouse gasses as driving 80 miles in a car. Beyond carbon emissions, they are also responsible for creating 20% of the world’s wastewater, through processes like dyeing and treating fabric.

So, what can you do to help? First, you can buy less clothes. Buy clothes that you need, and will wear often, not items you think you want but will be worn once before being forgotten in the back of your closet. Next, you can buy used or thrifted clothing. This will reduce the amount of clothing that goes to landfills, and your money won’t be supporting businesses that are unsustainably polluting the environment. Finally, you can buy clothing from sustainable brands, such as People Tree or Patagonia.
